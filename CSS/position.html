A paragraph is a group of sentences that develops a single idea. Paragraphs are a fundamental part of any piece of writing that is longer than a few sentences. They help the reader understand the structure of the writing and grasp its main points. Here are some characteristics of paragraphs: Topic sentence: A well-organized paragraph begins with a topic sentence that expresses the main idea. Supporting sentences: The remaining sentences in the paragraph support the main idea. Flow: The sentences in a paragraph should maintain a consistent flow. Length: There are no strict limits on the length of a paragraph, but it's usually three to five sentences. Organization: Paragraphs help the reader see where different sections of a piece of writing begin and end. Paragraphs can contain many different types of information, such as: A series of examples A long illustration A description of a place, character, or process A narration of events A comparison or contrast A classification of items A description of causes and effects The word "paragraph" comes from the Greek word paragraphos, which means "line used to mark change of persons in a dialogue".
